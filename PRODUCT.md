# Product

> Sprout Planner is the first module of **Sprout Universe**, a growing family of
> small, calm tools that share one design language. The public marketing
> landing page for the universe lives at `/` (`src/landing/`); the Planner
> module is served at `/app/`.

## Register

product

## Users

Sprout Planner is for people who want a gentle daily habit and task ritual on a personal device. They use it in short sessions: checking today's plan, marking tasks complete, reviewing streaks, and adjusting recurring monthly tasks or reminders.

## Product Purpose

The product helps users tend a small set of daily and monthly recurring tasks without the pressure of a heavy productivity suite. Success means the user can understand today at a glance, complete or add tasks quickly, and feel encouraged by progress, streaks, and calendar feedback.

## Brand Personality

Calm, encouraging, tactile. The app should feel like tending a small living terrarium: warm, clear, and quietly rewarding. It should support momentum without turning habits into a noisy game.

## Anti-references

Avoid corporate dashboard sameness, neon gamified streak apps, badge clutter, XP mechanics, dense admin-console chrome, dark tech gradients, and hard drop-shadow card stacks. Avoid decorative complexity that slows down the daily check-in.

## Design Principles

1. Put today's action first: the user should know what to do within two seconds.
2. Make progress feel alive: mascots, color, and motion should explain state, not decorate.
3. Keep the shell predictable: navigation, settings, and content regions should stay stable across pages.
4. Use restrained warmth: green is the identity, orange/amber/red are reserved for meaningful status.
5. Respect short sessions: controls must be thumb-friendly, readable, and efficient on a phone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Keep every interactive control at least 44px in one dimension, preserve visible focus states, support reduced motion, avoid color-only state communication, and maintain language-aware font stacks for English, Thai, Simplified Chinese, and Traditional Chinese.
